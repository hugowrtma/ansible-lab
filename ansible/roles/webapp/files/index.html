The Story

Formed in New York City in 1998, The Strokes emerged as the vanguard of the garage rock revival that would define the early 2000s. Childhood friends Julian Casablancas and Nick Valensi were soon joined by Nikolai Fraiture, Fabrizio Moretti, and Albert Hammond Jr., creating a lineup that would change the face of rock music.

Their 2001 debut album, Is This It, arrived like a shot of adrenaline to a stagnant rock scene. With its raw production, tight songwriting, and effortlessly cool aesthetic, the album became one of the most influential records of the 21st century. Critics hailed it as a masterpiece, and it inspired countless bands to pick up guitars again.

The band's sound—a blend of punk energy, new wave grooves, and Lou Reed-style deadpan vocals—felt both nostalgic and revolutionary. Songs like "Last Nite," "Someday," and "Hard to Explain" became anthems for a generation seeking authenticity in an increasingly manufactured pop landscape.

Over the years, The Strokes have continued to evolve, releasing six studio albums and maintaining their status as one of rock's most important bands. Despite tensions, lineup changes, and the inevitable challenges of longevity, they've remained vital, proving that their initial spark wasn't just a flash in the pan.

The Band

Julian Casablancas

Lead Vocals, Guitar

The frontman and primary songwriter, Julian's distinctive vocals and laid-back persona became synonymous with The Strokes' sound. His cigarette-and-whiskey delivery and poetic lyrics defined the band's aesthetic.

Nick Valensi

Lead Guitar

Nick's melodic guitar lines and solos provide much of The Strokes' sonic identity. His interplay with Albert Hammond Jr. creates the band's signature dual-guitar sound.

Albert Hammond Jr.

Rhythm Guitar

Son of songwriter Albert Hammond, Albert Jr. brings sophisticated chord progressions and has also pursued a successful solo career alongside his work with the band.

Nikolai Fraiture

Bass Guitar

The backbone of The Strokes' rhythm section, Nikolai's bass lines are both minimalist and essential, providing the groove that drives their songs.

Fabrizio Moretti

Drums

Fab's drumming combines punk energy with subtle technique, creating the propulsive beats that underpin The Strokes' most memorable tracks.

Discography

2001

Is This It

The album that started it all. Raw, urgent, and instantly classic. Featured "Last Nite," "Someday," and "Hard to Explain."

2003

Room on Fire

More polished but still fierce, with hits like "Reptilia" and "12:51." Proved they weren't a one-album wonder.

2006

First Impressions of Earth

A more ambitious, experimental effort featuring "Juicebox" and "Heart in a Cage." The band's longest album.

2011

Angles

After a five-year hiatus, The Strokes returned with new wave influences and tracks like "Under Cover of Darkness."

2013

Comedown Machine

A more subdued, exploratory album that showcased the band's willingness to take risks and experiment.

2020

The New Abnormal

Their triumphant return, winning a Grammy for Best Rock Album. Featured "Bad Decisions" and "The Adults Are Talking."

Legacy

The Strokes didn't just make great music—they changed the trajectory of rock music in the 21st century. Their influence can be heard in countless bands that followed, from Arctic Monkeys to The 1975, all owing a debt to the New York quintet's blueprint.

Beyond their musical impact, The Strokes represented a cultural shift. They brought back the idea that rock bands could be cool again, that guitar music still mattered, and that authenticity could triumph over polish. Their rumpled suits, vintage gear, and CBGB's aesthetic became the template for indie rock style.

Is This It consistently appears on "greatest albums of all time" lists, and their songs remain staples of alternative radio and streaming playlists. They've headlined major festivals worldwide and influenced everyone from bedroom recording artists to stadium rock bands.

More than two decades after their formation, The Strokes continue to record and perform, proving that the fire that made them special still burns. They remain a testament to the enduring power of great songwriting, tight musicianship, and undeniable chemistry.
